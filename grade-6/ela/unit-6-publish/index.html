Lesson 1

Looking Back: My Reading and Writing Journey

Review your work from the year, identify your growth, and prepare to build a portfolio that shows who you've become as a reader, writer, and communicator.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can reflect on my growth as a reader and writer by analyzing my own work.
  • I can identify strengths, growth areas, and favourite pieces from across the year.
  • I can use self-monitoring to evaluate my own comprehension and communication.
MetacognitionReflectionGrowth
Metacognition Thinking about your own thinking — being aware of how you learn and what strategies work for you. Portfolio A curated collection of your best work that shows growth and achievement over time. Revision The process of re-seeing and improving a piece of writing — not just fixing errors, but making it stronger. Self-Assessment Evaluating your own work and learning using criteria, rubrics, or reflection questions.

📖 Read: Looking Back to Move Forward

Before you build your final portfolio, take a moment to look back at the journey you've taken this year. In Grade 6 ELA, you have:

🪞

Unit 1

Explored identity through narrative writing and discussion circles

📕

Unit 2

Analyzed fiction craft and wrote your own original short story

🔎

Unit 3

Evaluated sources, conducted research, and wrote evidence-based reports

⚖️

Unit 4

Compared perspectives, built arguments, and engaged in dialogue

🌿

Unit 5

Created poetry, explored figurative language, and performed spoken word

🎓

Unit 6

Now: reflect, select, revise, and celebrate your growth

Key Insight: A portfolio isn't just a collection of finished work. It's a story of growth. The best portfolios show not just what you did well, but how you improved — what was hard, what you learned, and what you're proud of.

Growth Questions to Guide Your Review

  • Which piece of writing am I most proud of? Why?
  • Where did I struggle, and what did I learn from that struggle?
  • What comprehension or writing strategy made the biggest difference for me?
  • How has my voice as a writer changed from Unit 1 to now?
  • What would I do differently if I could redo one unit?

⏸️ Pause and Check

Without looking back at your work yet — what is one thing you remember being really proud of this year? What is one thing that challenged you?

There's no single right answer here! The goal is honest self-reflection. Something you're proud of might be your identity narrative, a spoken word performance, a research report, or even just a moment where you participated in a discussion for the first time. Challenges might include writing a longer piece, understanding figurative language, or presenting to others.
