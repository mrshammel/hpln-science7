Who Am I? Exploring Identity

Use comprehension strategies to explore identity-themed texts and make personal connections.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can use comprehension strategies (connecting, questioning, inferring) to understand a text.
  • I can identify explicit and implicit information in a text about identity.
  • I can make personal connections (text-to-self) while reading.
Comprehension Connections Identity
Identity Who you are — shaped by your experiences, relationships, culture, and beliefs. Text-to-Self When something in a text reminds you of your own life or feelings. Inference A conclusion based on evidence and reasoning — reading "between the lines." Explicit Information stated directly and clearly in the text. Implicit Information suggested or hinted at, not stated directly.

Key Comprehension Strategies for This Unit:

  • Connecting: What does this remind me of in my own life?
  • Questioning: What am I wondering about as I read?
  • Inferring: What is the author suggesting without saying directly?

Anchor Text: "The Name Jar" Excerpt

"Unhei stared at the name tag on her desk — the one her teacher had carefully written in English letters. It looked strange and unfamiliar. On the bus that morning, the other kids had stumbled over her name, repeating it wrong, laughing at the sounds. She wanted to disappear. But her grandmother's voice echoed in her memory: 'Your name means grace. It was chosen with love.'"

— Adapted from The Name Jar by Yangsook Choi

Words carry more than definitions. They carry culture, emotion, history, and identity. Understanding the deeper meaning of words helps us connect with texts — and with each other.

Key Vocabulary Strategies

🔎

Context Clues

Use surrounding words and sentences to figure out meaning.

🌳

Word Roots

Break words into parts (prefixes, roots, suffixes) to unlock meaning.

💭

Connotation

The feeling or association a word carries beyond its dictionary definition.

Identity Words in Context

Read these sentences from identity texts. Notice how each bolded word carries deep meaning:

  • "Her heritage was woven into every dish her grandmother prepared." — Heritage means the traditions, values, and culture passed down through generations.
  • "He felt a deep sense of belonging when he heard his family's language spoken." — Belonging means feeling accepted, connected, and at home.
  • "She wanted to reclaim her name after years of hearing it mispronounced." — Reclaim means to take back something that was lost or taken away.
  • "His resilience showed in the way he kept going despite the challenges." — Resilience means the ability to recover and stay strong through difficulty.
  • "The painting was a mosaic of her many identities — daughter, student, artist, friend." — Mosaic (figurative) means a combination of many different pieces forming one beautiful whole.
Heritage Traditions, values, and culture passed down through generations. Belonging Feeling accepted, connected, and at home in a group or place. Reclaim To take back something that was lost, dismissed, or taken away. Resilience The ability to recover and stay strong through difficulty. Mosaic A combination of many different pieces forming one beautiful whole (figurative). Connotation The emotional feeling or association a word carries beyond its definition.
