Lesson 1

Main Ideas and Supporting Details

Learn how to identify the main idea and supporting details in informational texts.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can identify the main idea of an informational text.
  • I can distinguish between the main idea and supporting details.
  • I can summarize the key points of a nonfiction text.
ComprehensionMain IdeaSummarizing
Main Idea The most important point the author is making — what the text is mostly about. Supporting Details Facts, examples, or evidence that explain, prove, or expand on the main idea. Summary A brief restatement of the most important ideas in a text, in your own words. Informational Text Nonfiction text written to inform, explain, or teach the reader about a topic.

🎬 Watch: Finding the Main Idea

📺 Educational Video — Main Idea (4 min)

📖 Read: Ocean Plastic — A Growing Problem

Every year, approximately 8 million tonnes of plastic enter the world's oceans. This plastic doesn't disappear — it breaks into smaller and smaller pieces called microplastics, which are now found in the water, sea life, and even the air we breathe.

Main Idea: Ocean plastic pollution is a serious and growing environmental problem that affects marine life, ecosystems, and human health.

Scientists have found microplastics in fish, sea turtles, and seabirds. When animals eat plastic, it can block their digestive systems or release toxic chemicals into their bodies. Coral reefs — already threatened by warming oceans — are also harmed when plastic settles on them.

Researchers are developing new solutions, from ocean cleanup machines to biodegradable packaging. But experts agree that the most effective solution is reducing plastic use at the source — using reusable containers, refusing single-use plastics, and improving recycling systems.

📌

Main Idea

Ocean plastic pollution is a growing crisis affecting wildlife and ecosystems.

📎

Details

8M tonnes/year, microplastics in animals, coral damage, cleanup solutions.

⏸️ Pause and Check

In your own words, what is the main idea of this text? Name two supporting details.

Main Idea: Plastic pollution in the ocean is a serious problem. Details: (1) 8 million tonnes enter oceans yearly; (2) microplastics harm fish, turtles, and seabirds.
