Lesson 1

What Makes a Great Story?

Analyze what draws readers into fiction. Use inference and visualization to understand character, conflict, and engagement.

🎯 Learning Targets

  • I can identify what makes fiction engaging and use inference to understand characters.
  • I can visualize scenes and explain how an author creates mental images.
  • I can identify character traits using text evidence.
Comprehension Inference Character Analysis
Protagonist The main character in a story — the one the reader follows and roots for. Antagonist A character or force that opposes the protagonist and creates conflict. Conflict The central problem or struggle in a story that drives the plot forward. Visualization Creating mental pictures while reading to deepen understanding. Character Traits Qualities that describe a character's personality, revealed through actions, words, and thoughts.

🎬 Watch: Elements of a Story

📺 Educational Video — Story Elements (5 min)

📖 Read: What Hooks a Reader?

Every great story begins with something that hooks the reader — a moment of tension, a question, or a vivid image that makes you want to keep reading. But what makes a story truly powerful is how the author builds conflict, develops characters, and uses language to create an experience.

What Great Stories Have:

  • A character you care about — someone with wants, fears, and flaws
  • A conflict that matters — something at stake for the character
  • Details that make you see, hear, and feel — vivid, sensory language
  • Change — the character is different by the end

Anchor Text Excerpt

"Mija crouched behind the dumpster, her heart slamming against her ribs. The stray dog — the one with the torn ear and the limp — pressed against her leg, trembling. She could hear the older kids on the other side, kicking at cans, calling out. She wrapped her arm around the dog's neck and whispered, 'It's okay. I won't let them hurt you.' Her voice was braver than she felt."

👤

Character

Mija — brave, protective, compassionate. She acts despite her fear.

⚡

Conflict

Mija must protect the stray dog from the threatening older kids.

🎨

Craft

"Heart slamming," "torn ear," "braver than she felt" — vivid imagery and irony.

⏸️ Pause and Check

Re-read the anchor text. What can you infer about Mija's character? What text clues support your answer?

Inference: Mija is courageous and empathetic. Even though she's scared (heart slamming, "braver than she felt"), she stays to protect something vulnerable. The contrast between her fear and her words shows inner strength. Her action of whispering reassurance to the dog reveals compassion.
